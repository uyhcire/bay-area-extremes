Somewhere in the public data, the Bay Area is the most extreme place in the country at something weird.

Highest this. Lowest that. The widest gap, the steepest curve. The data already knows — it's just buried in a hundred-million-row spreadsheet that nobody has time to open.

05 — The game

Three rules, one win condition.

Pick any measurable dimension. If a Bay Area metro lands at either end of the leaderboard, it's an entry.

Finding specextremal ✓
SubjectA Bay Area metro — the SF, San Jose, or combined Bay CSA
BenchmarkThe 20 largest U.S. metros (MSAs) or combined areas (CSAs)
DimensionAnything in the data — income, commute, rent, age, industry mix…
WinRank #1 or #20 — the max or the min. Outliers welcome.
06 — Ground rules

What counts as the Bay?

MSA — a metro statistical area, one urban core. CSA — a combined area that links adjacent metros. The Bay shows up as a few of each; pick the one your dimension fits.
